# Commercial & industry relations — stakeholder playbook

The pilot shops, the industry bodies, and the channels that reach shop owners. The rollout plan
owns the phase sequencing and recruiting profiles; this playbook owns the per-stakeholder prep so
each conversation starts warm and specific. Status of record:
[human-decisions.md](../human-decisions.md) rows **V-04** (pilot rehearsal/load) and **H-12**
(the still-open contract/intake flow); phase exits live in [rollout.md](../rollout.md).

## Why this matters (impact, not blockage)

Unlike legal or ops, nothing here has a hard external clock except **DEMA (decide by early
September)** — but everything here compounds: a pilot recruited this week is a case study by
DEMA, and a case study by DEMA is Phase 3's press hook. The risk is sequencing, not paperwork:
marketing ahead of evidence violates the [claims policy](../marketing.md) and turns
differentiators into liabilities.

## Who to talk to, with what in hand

### Design-partner shop owners (2–3, recruit now)

The three profiles and the channel list are specified in
[rollout.md](../rollout.md#who-to-recruit--three-deliberate-profiles) — don't re-derive them.
What to have prepared per conversation:

- **The one-pager** stating [the offer](../rollout.md#the-offer-write-it-down-say-it-the-same-way-every-time)
  in the rollout's exact terms: free through the pilot, founder-run concierge migration (they
  send exports; we never log into their incumbent system — the
  [portability-wedge legal guardrail](../assessments/competitive-strategy.md#the-portability-wedge)),
  weekly 30-minute call, direct founder thread, founding price + two-year lock on conversion.
  In exchange: real days, observation access, and (if earned) a named case study.
- **The pilot agreement** to sign ([legal.md](legal.md)'s contract set — do not run a pilot on a
  handshake once real diver data is involved).
- **The insurer one-pager** ([insurance.md](insurance.md)) — expect "does this affect my
  coverage?" in conversation one.
- **The dock demo:** the demo shop on a phone, rehearsed (V-04's dry run is the rehearsal). For
  an EVE/DiveShop360 defector, additionally: their incumbent's export walkthrough from the
  live `/switching` guide, so the migration feels concrete.
- **Etiquette for the public channels** (ScubaBoard threads, Facebook shop-owner groups): respond
  to named problems with specifics; never broadcast. These threads are where the
  [competitive research](../assessments/competitive-strategy.md) mined incumbent complaints —
  the credibility only works if replies stay in that register.

### Pilot shops' front-desk staff and captains (Phase 1, week 0)

The actual daily users. Prepared: the week-0 training session plan (one hour, their real
upcoming week already imported), the V-04 rehearsal checklist run against that week, and the
founder's availability commitment for the first boat day (which doubles as V-02 evidence if not
yet recorded — [dive-operations.md](dive-operations.md)).

### DEMA (decision by early September)

Membership is worth it regardless (retailer research, legitimacy). The decision is posture:
booth economics vs. walking the floor with pre-booked meetings + a founding-shop evening — the
rollout [already leans meetings-first](../rollout.md#phase-3--public-launch-november-2026-onward)
for a one-founder company. Prepared for the decision: booth cost quote, a target meeting list
(founding shops, switching-pool prospects, media), and the calendar reality of November.

### Scubanomics / Darcy Kieran; dive-media editors (Divernet, DeeperBlue, X-Ray Mag)

Warm up in Phase 2 with **useful content, not pitches** (the rollout's phrasing). Prepared
before any outreach: the published case study with real numbers, the story angle per outlet
(Divernet already covered the EVE acquisition — "what happens to EVE users now" is their
natural follow-up; Scubanomics gets the business-of-diving economics angle), and the visual
(roll call on a wet phone — only after V-02 evidence exists).

### Founding-shop references (Phase 2, day-30 check-ins)

Prepared: the Capterra listing claimed *before* asking anyone for a review (first product in
this niche with five real reviews wins the comparison page — see
[rollout Phase 2](../rollout.md#phase-2--founding-shops-oct-2026--dema)), a one-link review ask,
the case-study interview template (their numbers, their words, sign-off on named quotes —
consent lives in the pilot agreement), and the shop-to-shop referral offer (a free month both
sides).
